# Parent and Teacher Guide

## Purpose

This repository helps students learn AI in a structured, safe, and academically honest way. It is not designed to encourage shortcut learning. It is designed to teach students how to ask better questions, verify information, prepare for exams, and create projects responsibly.

## Suggested supervision by age

| Grade band | Supervision suggestion |
|---|---|
| Grade 3 to 5 | Use only with parent or teacher support. Focus on stories, quizzes, vocabulary, and simple explanations. |
| Grade 6 to 8 | Allow guided independent use. Review privacy and academic honesty rules. |
| Grade 9 to 12 | Encourage disciplined use for revision, research support, presentations, coding, and career exploration. Discuss bias, sources, and ethics. |

## Recommended rules for home or classroom

1. Students must try first before asking AI for answers.
2. AI may explain, quiz, and give feedback, but should not complete assignments dishonestly.
3. Students must not upload personal or sensitive information.
4. Students should maintain a mistake notebook.
5. Students should be asked to explain what they learned without AI.
6. For major projects, students should disclose how AI was used if required.

## Classroom activity format

A 60-minute class can follow this pattern:

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5 minutes | Story introduction. |
| 10 minutes | Concept explanation. |
| 10 minutes | Tool demonstration. |
| 15 minutes | Student activity. |
| 10 minutes | Reflection and discussion. |
| 10 minutes | Safety or ethics checkpoint. |

## Assessment idea

Do not assess students only on the final AI-assisted output. Assess:

- Clarity of prompt.
- Evidence of student thinking.
- Accuracy after verification.
- Ethical use.
- Reflection on mistakes.
- Ability to explain without AI.
