# Student Safety and Ethics Guide

## First rule

Do not share private information with AI tools.

Private information includes:

- Full address.
- Phone number.
- Passwords.
- School ID numbers.
- Aadhaar or government ID details.
- Private photos.
- Family financial details.
- Medical details.
- Personal chats.
- Private school documents.
- Anything your parent or teacher would not want shared online.

## The THINK test before using AI

| Letter | Question |
|---|---|
| T | Is this task allowed by my teacher? |
| H | Have I tried to think first? |
| I | Is the information I am sharing safe? |
| N | Need to verify facts? |
| K | Know how I will use the answer honestly? |

## How to use AI ethically for school

Good:

```text
Explain this concept to me.
```

Good:

```text
Give me hints, not the final answer.
```

Good:

```text
Check my answer and tell me where I can improve.
```

Not good:

```text
Write my assignment so I can submit it as mine.
```

Not good:

```text
Make fake references for my project.
```

Not good:

```text
Give me a way to cheat in my test.
```

## Hallucination checklist

Before trusting an AI answer, ask:

1. Does my textbook say the same thing?
2. Can my teacher confirm it?
3. Does a reliable source support it?
4. Is the answer too confident without proof?
5. Did AI invent names, dates, formulas, or quotes?

## Bias checklist

Ask AI:

```text
What assumptions might be hidden in this answer? Give me another perspective and tell me what I should verify.
```

## Healthy use limits

AI should not replace:

- Reading.
- Writing practice.
- Mental maths.
- Handwriting.
- Teacher feedback.
- Class attention.
- Real friendship.
- Outdoor play.
- Sleep.
